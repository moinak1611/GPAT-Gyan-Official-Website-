Pharmacology Syllabus

General Pharmacology

Principles of drug action, pharmacokinetics, and pharmacodynamics.

Systemic Pharmacology

Pharmacological effects on cardiovascular, respiratory, gastrointestinal, and central nervous systems.

Chemotherapy

Pharmacology of antimicrobial and anticancer agents.

Toxicology

Study of adverse effects of drugs and poisons.

×

Pharmaceutics Syllabus

Physical Pharmacy

Topics include matter properties, micromeritics, surface and interfacial phenomena, rheology, and dispersion systems.

Pharmaceutical Technology

Covers formulation and development of dosage forms such as tablets, capsules, parenterals, and novel drug delivery systems.

Biopharmaceutics and Pharmacokinetics

Studies on drug absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion, bioavailability, and bioequivalence.

×

Pharmaceutical Chemistry Syllabus

Inorganic Pharmaceutical Chemistry

Study of pharmaceutical impurities, monographs, isotopes, and agents like dentifrices and anticaries.

Organic Chemistry

Focus on general principles, different classes of compounds, aromaticity, stereochemistry, and pericyclic reactions.

Medicinal Chemistry

Exploration of therapeutic classes of drugs, their mechanisms, and structure-activity relationships.

Pharmaceutical Analysis

Analytical techniques including titrations, spectroscopy, chromatography, and quality control methods.

Biochemistry

Cell structure, biomolecules, enzymology, and metabolic pathways.

×

Pharmacognosy Syllabus

Basics of Pharmacognosy

Introduction to crude drugs, classification, and factors affecting their quality.

Phytochemistry

Study of bioactive constituents like alkaloids, glycosides, tannins, and volatile oils.

Herbal Drugs

Focus on traditional systems of medicine, herbal formulations, and standardization.

×

Allied Subject Syllabus

Microbial classification, sterilization techniques, immunology, and microbial assays.

Pharmaceutical Jurisprudence

Laws and ethics governing the pharmacy profession in India.

Clinical Pharmacy and Therapeutics

Drug interactions, adverse drug reactions, clinical trials, and patient counseling.

Human Anatomy and Physiology

Structure and function of human body systems.

Pharmaceutical Engineering

Unit operations, material handling, and pharmaceutical plant design.

Pharmaceutical Management

Principles of management, entrepreneurship, and pharmaceutical marketing.
